Water – A Precious Natural Resource

Water is one of the most important natural resources on Earth. All living things need water to survive. We use water for drinking, cooking, cleaning, farming and many other activities. In this part, we will learn about the uses, sources and conservation of water.

Uses of Water

  • We use water for drinking, cooking and washing.
  • Farmers need water to grow crops and plants.
  • Water is used in factories to make many products.
  • Water is needed for animals, birds and all living things.

Sources & Saving of Water

  • Water comes from rain, rivers, lakes, ponds and wells.
  • Groundwater is stored under the soil and taken out using pumps and wells.
  • We should not waste water while brushing, bathing or washing.
  • Rainwater harvesting and fixing leaking taps help in saving water.

Importance of Air for Living Things

Air is all around us, even though we cannot see it. It is a mixture of gases and is very important for life. Humans, animals and plants all depend on air in different ways. In this part, we will see how air helps living things and what it is made of.

How Air Helps Living Things

  • Oxygen present in air helps humans and animals to breathe.
  • Plants take carbon dioxide from air to make their food (photosynthesis).
  • Air helps in burning; without air, a candle will not burn.
  • Moving air (wind) helps in pollination and seed dispersal in plants.

What is Air Made Of?

  • Air is a mixture of many gases.
  • It has mostly nitrogen and oxygen, and small amounts of other gases.
  • Clean air is important for a healthy life.
  • We should reduce air pollution by planting trees and avoiding burning waste.

Garbage & Waste Management

Every day, we throw away many things like paper, plastic covers, food peels and bottles. This waste is called garbage. If garbage is not managed properly, it can make our surroundings dirty and harm the environment. In this part, we will learn about types of waste and the 3R rule – Reduce, Reuse and Recycle.

Types of Waste & 3R Rule

  • Biodegradable waste: fruit peels, vegetable waste and paper that can rot and mix with soil.
  • Non-biodegradable waste: plastic, glass and metal that do not rot easily.
  • Reduce: use less plastic and avoid unnecessary packaging.
  • Reuse and Recycle: use things again and convert waste into useful products.

Good Habits for Waste Management

  • Use separate dustbins for wet waste (food) and dry waste (plastic, paper).
  • Do not throw garbage on roads, in rivers or open places.
  • Use cloth or jute bags instead of plastic bags while shopping.
  • Join clean-up activities in school and neighbourhood to keep the environment clean.
